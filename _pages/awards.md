I am a first-year Ph.D student in the Electrical & Computer Engineering department at the University of Wisconsin - Madison. My area of research is in power electronics, particularly in matrix converter topologies applied to a variety of applications such as isolated motor drives, switched power amplifiers and single-wire earth return power transmission. 

### Background
Originally from [Kolkata, India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolkata), I spent all twelve years of formal schooling in [Chennai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chennai) and a few years after in [Pune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pune). As might be obvious, I speak multiple Indian languages! I got my Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering from the [Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology, Shibpur](https://www.iiests.ac.in/) formerly known as _Bengal Engineering & Science University_. Interested in power electronics research, I decided to pursue a Masters in ECE at WEMPEC, UW-Madison
